Website Redesign

OVERVIEW

The Duquesne Incline noted that their uses had trouble navigating their website and were not able to locate the information they needed.

To solve this problem I conducted user research to locate the exact problems users were having. Using my findings, I created a new website using HTML, CSS, and Javascript that is more user-friendly.

ROLE

UX Researcher
Prototyping & Testing
Web Developer
Web Designer

DURATION

March 2021 - May 2021

Problem

COLOR SCHEME

The website does not follow a consistant use of colors and tends to have a different color for everything. This makes reading through the website difficult.

TEXT

The text is also in different colors and in different fonts. Some of the more important texts are in lighter shades which makes it easy for users to miss. Additionally, parts of the website are written in large bulks of text that are not easily skimmable. Moreover, there is no heirarchy used to organize the information on each page.

IMAGES

The Duquesne Incline has some of the most beautiful videos of Pittsburgh, however, the images used on the website are of bad quality and do not represent the incline well.

REPITITION

Multiple chunks of texts are repeated multiple times throughout the website. Even though there is a menu for different pages, most pages contain the same or similar information.
